FRONT CURTAINS

2.10 m clear opening · with purple cover drape

The cream tie-back curtains can’t be removed. A brand drape hung in front hides them — it takes ~450 mm off each side (vs ~300 mm for the tie-backs alone), so the 3.0 m front narrows to about 2.1 m.

WHAT’S ON THE STAND

  1. 1Hoisted main banner — new print on a free-standing timber frame that holds it high, above the bunting. Nothing fixed to the tent, and the stand front stays open — no barrier.
  2. 2Tension-fabric media wall (2.4 m wide) with built-in LED spotlights. Sits to the left, leaving a 600 mm access gap on the right to slip behind. Doubles as the divider.
  3. 3Screen on a dressed table — the Mobile Pixels sits on a 700×700 mm table with a floor-length brand tablecloth; the printed cut-out surround wraps the screen with “Take a Stand Against Youth Homelessness” above. Lifts off each night.
  4. 4Two pull-up banners (850 mm each) — “Behind Every Hero” + “Be the Hero of Your Own Story”, against a white shimmery fabric that softens the left wall.
  5. 5Pop-up desk + donation bucket — at the entry gap, angled to greet not block.
  6. 6Single-column leaflet stand — out at the front of the gazebo, just before the A-frame, to grab passers-by.
  7. 7“Step Up” game — pulled out in front of the tent to draw people in. Placeholder — swap for whatever game you land on.
  8. 8False back + staff area — the media wall sits on the 2 m line; garden box behind it is your seat/desk and storage.
  9. 9A-frame board — out on the walkway to catch attention and point people in.
